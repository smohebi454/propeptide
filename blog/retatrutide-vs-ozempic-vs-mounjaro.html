Retatrutide vs Ozempic vs Mounjaro: Why the Triple Agonist Changes Everything

March 9, 2026 — Owais Mohebi

Ozempic and Mounjaro are widely available in Dubai pharmacies. They work. Patients lose weight. But a growing body of clinical evidence reveals a persistent problem: users often experience significant muscle loss alongside fat loss. Retatrutide addresses these limitations through a fundamentally different mechanism — not just because it produces more weight loss (it does, substantially). But because of how it produces that weight loss, and what it might spare in the process.

Three Main Problems With Current GLP-1 Medications

1. Muscle Loss

With existing GLP-1 medications, roughly 20–40% of the weight you lose is lean mass. These drugs rely primarily on appetite suppression, pushing you into a caloric deficit. The body loses weight, but it does not discriminate — muscle tissue goes along with fat. There is no built-in mechanism to preserve lean mass.

2. Anhedonia (Emotional Blunting)

GLP-1 receptors exist in brain regions that control reward and pleasure. Strong receptor suppression can dampen not just appetite but emotional responsiveness more broadly. Some users report a persistent emotional flatness — reduced enjoyment of food, social activities, and daily life. This is not a rare side effect; it is a direct consequence of how aggressively these drugs suppress reward circuitry.

3. Weight Loss Plateaus

Semaglutide users typically plateau at around 15% total body weight loss by weeks 60–68. Tirzepatide pushes further, with plateaus around 20–22%. In both cases, the body adapts. Metabolic rate adjusts downward, appetite suppression becomes less effective, and weight loss stalls. The single- and dual-agonist approach hits a ceiling.

Mechanism Comparison

  • Semaglutide (Ozempic/Wegovy): Single GLP-1 receptor agonist. Suppresses appetite via one pathway.
  • Tirzepatide (Mounjaro/Zepbound): Dual agonist targeting GLP-1 and GIP receptors. Two pathways for appetite and insulin signalling.
  • Retatrutide: Triple agonist targeting GLP-1, GIP, and glucagon receptors. Three distinct pathways — appetite suppression, insulin regulation, and direct energy expenditure.

The critical difference is the third receptor. Glucagon receptor activation introduces a mechanism that neither Ozempic nor Mounjaro can replicate.

The Glucagon Advantage

Glucagon receptor activation increases resting energy expenditure independently of other factors. Rather than relying solely on eating less, retatrutide causes the body to burn more energy at rest. This shifts the balance: instead of the body simply receiving fewer calories and losing whatever tissue it can, the glucagon pathway drives the body to mobilise fat stores specifically for energy.

This is not theoretical. It is the reason retatrutide produces a different body composition outcome than its predecessors.

Body Composition Data

A substudy published in The Lancet Diabetes & Endocrinology (2025) examined body composition changes using DXA scans — the gold standard for distinguishing fat loss from lean mass loss. The results showed that retatrutide produced preferential fat loss. The ratio of fat-to-lean tissue lost was significantly better than what has been observed with semaglutide or tirzepatide in comparable studies.

This is the data point that changes the conversation. It is one thing to lose more total weight. It is another to lose predominantly fat while retaining muscle.

Side Effects

All three compounds share gastrointestinal side effects: nausea, vomiting, diarrhoea, and constipation. These are inherent to GLP-1 receptor agonism and appear across the class.

With retatrutide, Phase 2 data showed that starting at a lower initial dose (2mg versus 4mg) nearly halved the incidence of GI side effects. This dose-titration approach appears to allow the body to adjust to triple-receptor activation without the sharp onset of symptoms seen at higher starting doses.

The side effect profile is manageable and broadly comparable to existing treatments when titration is handled correctly.

The Bottom Line

Retatrutide matters not just because it produces more weight loss. But because of how it produces that weight loss. Ozempic and Mounjaro change your inputs — they make you eat less. Retatrutide changes the code. It alters what your body does with the energy it already has, driving it to burn fat directly while preserving the lean tissue that existing medications sacrifice.

That is the difference between a drug that suppresses appetite and one that reprogrammes metabolism.
